to Active Participant.

Every student goes through this clear onboarding process before they become a full group member.

1

Click the invite link and enter the Nexus server. Open to all students in Australia.

Open to all students
2

📖 Read the Introduction

Start in #welcome and read what Nexus is, how it works, and what it offers.

Required reading
3

📋 Read the Rules & Guidelines

Community standards

First connection point

Group Structure

Every Group Has a Student Leader.

Groups aren't self-managing. Each has a named student leader who takes responsibility for the community inside their group.

👑
The Student Group Leader

Each discussion group is managed by a student. Nexus has no teacher or school involvement. The leader shapes the group's quality, culture, and membership.

📝
Review Applications

Reads incoming applications and evaluates cultural fit.

✅
Accept or Decline Members

Makes the final call on membership. Keeps groups focused.

🗣️
Help Organise Discussions

Suggests topics, prompts conversation, keeps the group active.

🛡️
Enforce Group Standards

Upholds community guidelines. Escalates issues to admin when necessary.

📋 The Application & Review Flow
1
Student discovers a group in a domain
2
Student writes their application message
3
Application is sent to the group leader
4
Group leader reads and considers the application
5
Leader makes an accept / decline decision
6
Student is notified on Discord
✅ Accepted
Full group member
✗ Declined
Try a different group

💡 Why applications?
Open-access groups lose quality fast. A short application means members are genuinely invested, and the leader can shape the kind of community they want to build.

The Admin Team

Managing the ecosystem.
Fully student-run.

Nexus has no school involvement. Admins are students who keep the platform healthy, connect communities to the world, and handle safety — entirely independently.

📤 How Domain Leaders Talk to Admins
1Domain Leader meets with all Group Leaders in their domain
2Collects member feedback, visions, and emerging needs across all groups
3If an NPO session, inter-school exchange, or external collaboration is needed — compiles a clear proposal
→Submits the proposal to the Admin Team, who action it through external networks
🌐

Act on Domain Leader Proposals

When a Domain Leader submits a vision — e.g. "our Poverty group wants an NGO session" — admins find the partner and make it happen.

👩‍🏫

Connection to Teachers & School

Formal link between student communities and school staff — sharing updates, escalating concerns, coordinating where needed.

🤝

Cross-Community Collaboration

When Domain Leaders want to collaborate with another community or group, admins coordinate joint discussions, debates, and projects.

🛡️

Safety Oversight

Enforces community-wide guidelines and acts as the final escalation point when moderation issues arise.

Offline & Real World

Online first. Real life next.

Nexus is a digital home base — but we're 100% behind every student who takes the conversation off-screen. Go anywhere. Talk to anyone.

☕
Meet anywhere. Talk about anything.

While Nexus connects groups with NPOs, other communities, and external organisations, we know the best conversations are often the most casual ones. You don't need a formal event or official structure to make something real happen.

Just want to chat? Do it. Grab a coffee together, head to the beach, find a community space, meet at the library, hang out at the park. Whether it's a structured project meeting or two friends from the philosophy group talking on the bus — we're fully behind all of it. No forms. No permission. No formality needed.

Wherever feels right:

☕ Café 🏖️ Beach 📍 Community space 🌳 Park 📚 Library 🏠 Someone's place 🛤️ Anywhere you like

Nexus is the spark. You choose where the fire burns.

☕

Casual Catch-Ups

Coffee, lunch, after school — no structure needed. Just show up and talk.

🎤

Organised Events

Host a debate, reading session, or pitch night at a community space, library, or café — no permission needed.

🤝

Cross-Community Events

Admin-facilitated events connecting members from different communities and regions, via Domain Leader proposals.

We Actively Push Offline Engagement

The Nexus admin team doesn't just permit offline activity — we actively encourage and facilitate it. We'll help connect the right people, suggest ideas, and support your plans at any scale. Whether it's a casual café catch-up or a big community event, we're behind it completely.

🏃 Starting small is perfect. Message someone from your group. Get a coffee. Talk philosophy at the beach. The platform is the starting point — the real community is what happens when you show up.

Platform Safety

Safety is Our First Priority.

The Nexus admin team is committed to maintaining a safe, respectful, and responsible digital environment for every student on the platform.

🛡️

Admin Commitment to Online Safety

The Nexus admin team places safety above everything else. Nexus is a fully independent, student-run community with no school affiliation. We operate in line with Australian government online safety guidelines — including the Online Safety Act 2021 and the eSafety Commissioner's frameworks.

🇦🇺   Aligned with the Online Safety Act 2021 & eSafety Commissioner Guidelines
🔒
Closed Community

Student-verified community. Invite links are controlled and never made public. No anonymous or unverified members.

👁️
Active Admin Oversight

The admin team actively monitors the platform. All channels are observable. Issues are acted on promptly. Moderation is handled entirely by the student admin team.

📋
Clear Community Guidelines

All members agree to explicit guidelines on joining — informed by eSafety Commissioner best-practice standards for student online communities.

👑
Accountable Group Leaders

Every group has a named student leader accountable for conduct, with admin oversight above them at all times.

🚨
Rapid Moderation Response

Harmful content, harassment, or policy violations are addressed swiftly. Discord's moderation tools combined with active admin intervention.

🤝
Admin-Managed Safety

Safety is handled entirely by the Nexus admin team. There is no school involvement. Serious concerns are escalated through official online safety channels (eSafety Commissioner).

✓

No harassment, bullying, or targeted behaviour of any kind — online or referencing offline interactions

✓

No sharing of personal information beyond first name and year level without explicit consent

✓

Age-appropriate content only — all discussions are designed to be respectful and appropriate

✓

Respectful disagreement encouraged — personal attacks are never tolerated

✓

Reporting tools available to all members — any student can flag a concern to the admin team directly

✓

Platform activity reviewed by the admin team — all channels are observable and moderated by student admins

🇦🇺

Nexus is designed to align with the eSafety Commissioner's Safety by Design principles and the Australian Government's Online Safety Act 2021. We are committed to continuous improvement in line with national guidelines. Learn more at esafety.gov.au.
