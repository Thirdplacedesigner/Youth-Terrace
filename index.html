What is Nexus?

A new kind of space for Sandringham students.

"A platform where curiosity meets community — and discussion leads to action."

Nexus is a digital space built exclusively for Sandringham College students. It's organised around your real interests — not your year level or classes. Join the groups that actually excite you, talk to people who think like you, and go deeper than a classroom ever allows.

💬

Real Discussion

Structured conversations on topics that actually matter to you — not just small talk.

🔗

New Connections

Meet students across year levels who share your curiosity and passion.

🚀

Student-Led

Every group is run by a student leader. You set the agenda. You drive the discussions.

🛡️

Safe & Structured

An admin team maintains safety, guidelines, and a link with the school community.

🌐

Discord-Based Platform

Nexus lives on Discord — an app students already know and use. No new software, no learning curve.

🗂️

Organised by Interest Domains

The server is divided into broad areas of interest — Social Discussion, Art, Sports, Startups, and more. Each domain contains focused discussion groups.

📝

Application-Based Groups

You don't automatically enter a group. You apply with a short message, and a student leader reviews your request. This keeps groups tight-knit and high quality.

🤝

Admin Team as a Bridge

An admin team doesn't run discussions — they connect student communities with teachers, external organisations, and other schools.

Community Structure

Three Layers. One Platform.

Nexus is organised into three clear layers — from the whole school community down to focused discussion groups.

Layer 1 · The Platform
⚡ Nexus — Sandringham College

The overarching community for all Sandringham College students. One shared Discord server that everyone enters first.

↓
Layer 2 · Interest Domains
🗂️ 11 Interest Areas — Each with a Domain Leader

Each domain has a Domain Leader — a student who oversees all discussion groups within that area. They listen to their group leaders, collect emerging needs and visions, and act as the voice of the domain when connecting upward to the Admin Team.

How Students Join

8 Steps from New Member
to Active Participant.

Every student goes through this clear onboarding process before they become a full group member.

1

🔗 Join the Discord Server

The student clicks the invite link and enters the Nexus server for the first time. No application needed — the server is open to all Sandringham students.

Open to all SC students
2

📖 Read the Introduction

They start in #welcome and read the explanation of what Nexus is, how it works, and what it offers. This builds context before anything else.

Required reading
3

📋 Read the Rules & Guidelines

They review the community guidelines in #rules. Understanding what's expected keeps the platform respectful and high-quality for everyone.

Community standards
4

👋 Write a Self-Introduction

The student posts in #introduce-yourself — sharing who they are, their interests, and what they're hoping to find in Nexus. This is their first real community interaction.

First connection point
5

🗺️ Explore Interest Domains

They browse the different domain categories — reading channel descriptions, looking at ongoing conversations, and getting a feel for each community's vibe and culture.

Browse freely
6

🎯 Find a Group to Apply For

Once they've found a discussion group that genuinely interests them, they identify it as the one they want to be part of — a specific group within a specific domain.

Interest match
7

📝 Submit a Short Application

The student writes a short message explaining why they want to join that specific group — what they'd bring to conversations and why this topic matters to them.

Written by the student
8

✅ Group Leader Reviews & Decides

The student group leader reads the application and makes a decision — accept or decline. If accepted, the student becomes a full member of the group and can participate in all discussions.

✓ Accepted → Full member ✗ Declined → Try another group
Group Structure

Every Group Has a Student Leader.

Groups aren't self-managing. Each has a named student leader who takes responsibility for the community inside their group.

👑
The Student Group Leader

Each discussion group is managed by a student — not a teacher. The leader is a community member who takes on responsibility for the group's quality, culture, and membership.

📝
Review Applications

Reads incoming applications and evaluates whether the student would be a good fit for the group's culture and discussions.

✅
Accept or Decline Members

Makes the final call on membership. Keeps group sizes manageable and discussions focused.

🗣️
Help Organise Discussions

Suggests topics, prompts conversation when things go quiet, and keeps the group engaged and active.

🛡️
Enforce Group Standards

Upholds community guidelines within the group. Escalates issues to the admin team when necessary.

💡 Why applications?
Open-access groups lose quality fast. By requiring a short application, groups stay focused, members are genuinely invested, and the leader can shape the kind of community they want to build.

The Admin Team

Managing the ecosystem,
not the discussion.

Admins don't tell groups what to talk about. They keep the whole platform healthy, connected, and linked to the world beyond the server. Critically, they receive structured proposals — not noise — from Domain Leaders.

While Nexus primarily operates as a digital platform, students are strongly encouraged to meet and discuss ideas offline as well. Groups are free to organise conversations or collaborative sessions in spaces where they feel comfortable — such as areas inside the school, cafés, libraries, parks, or even the beach. Nexus actively supports these real-world discussions because meaningful conversations often grow stronger when people can meet face-to-face.

📤 How Domain Leaders Talk to Admins
1 Domain Leader meets with all Group Leaders in their domain
2 Collects member feedback, visions, and emerging needs across all groups
While Nexus helps coordinate collaborations with NPOs and other schools through the platform, groups are also encouraged to hold informal discussions offline whenever they want. Students can freely meet inside school spaces, cafés, or community areas to continue conversations in a relaxed and natural environment.
3 If an NPO session, inter-school exchange, or external collaboration is needed — compiles a clear proposal
→ Submits the proposal to the Admin Team, who action it through external networks
🌐

Act on Domain Leader Proposals

When a Domain Leader submits a vision — e.g. "our Poverty group wants to run a session with an NGO" — admins find the partner and make it happen.

👩‍🏫

Connection to Teachers & School

Admins serve as the formal link between student communities and school staff — sharing updates, escalating concerns, and coordinating where needed.

🏫

Inter-School Exchange

When Domain Leaders request cross-school interactions, admins coordinate with other schools to set up joint discussions, debates, or collaborative projects.

⚙️
🇦🇺

Aligned with Australian Online Safety Principles

The Nexus admin team places the safety of students on the digital platform as the highest priority. Community moderation and platform rules are designed to align with recognised Australian online safety guidelines to ensure respectful communication and responsible digital participation.

Platform Management

Creating domains, onboarding Domain Leaders and Group Leaders, and keeping the server structure clean and navigable.

🛡️

Safety Oversight

Admins enforce community-wide guidelines and act as the final escalation point when issues arise at the group or domain level.
